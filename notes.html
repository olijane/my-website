Notes on the Bassoon

Keys and Holes

The bassoon uses a mix of keys and holes to produce different pitches. It has five main holes on the with a sixth that is covered using a key. There are 17 additional keys on the front and back of the bassoon.

There 11 (12 for professional bassoons) thumb keys alone!

Range

The range of a bassoon begins at B♭1 and extends to G5. This means the bassoon can play roughly three octaves.

Half Holes and Quarter Holes

For the notes F♯, A♭, A, and high G♭ through high C require either a half hole or quarter hole.

This means that you have to shift your left hand pointer finger to only cover the first tone hole half-way or a quarter of the way. How much you cover the hole depends on your bassoon.

Flicking

Middle F♯, G, A♭, A, and B♭ tend to crack at the start of the note on many bassoons. To create a smoother transition, bassoonists can use the flicking keys to get rid of the crack.
