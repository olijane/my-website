The Bassoon

Overview

The bassoon is a musical instrument in the woodwind family, often playing in the tenor and bass ranges. It is composed of six pieces, including the bocal and reed. Music for the bassoon is usually written in bass and tenor clef.

There are two forms of modern bassoons: the Buffet (French) and Heckel (German) systems. First appearing in its modern form in the 19th century, bassoons feature prominently in orchestra, concert band, and chamber music.
